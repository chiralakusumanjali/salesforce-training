 Learnings – Day 02

* Gained a clear understanding of the Salesforce Platform and its importance in cloud-based CRM and business application development.
* Explored how Salesforce helps organizations manage customer relationships, automate workflows, and improve business efficiency.
* Learned the structure of Salesforce using Apps, Objects, Fields, Records, and Tabs.
* Understood that Apps are used to organize related business functionalities and modules in one place.
* Learned how Objects are used to store and manage data similar to database tables.
* Studied the relationship between Fields and Records within Salesforce Objects.
* Explored how Tabs help users easily navigate between objects, reports, and dashboards.
* Learned the purpose of Salesforce navigation tools such as App Launcher, Search Bar, and Navigation Menu.
* Understood the difference between Declarative Development and Programmatic Development in Salesforce.
* Learned that Declarative Development uses no-code tools for faster and simpler customization.
* Explored configuration tools such as Flow Builder, Validation Rules, Approval Processes, and Page Layouts.
* Studied how Apex programming is used to implement advanced business logic and automation.
* Gained basic knowledge of APIs and their role in integrating Salesforce with external systems and applications.
* Understood the concept of cloud computing and how Salesforce delivers services through the cloud.
* Learned the importance of Multi-Tenant Architecture and secure data isolation between organizations.
* Explored the roles and responsibilities of Salesforce Administrators and Salesforce Developers.
* Understood how CRM concepts such as Accounts, Contacts, Opportunities, and Cases are represented using Salesforce Objects.
* Learned how real-world business applications can be designed and managed using Apps, Objects, and automation tools.
* Improved understanding of Salesforce customization, workflow automation, and platform scalability.
* Learned how Salesforce combines configuration tools and coding technologies to build flexible enterprise-level solutions.
