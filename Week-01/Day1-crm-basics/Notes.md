Salesforce Training – Day 1 Notes

1. Introduction to Salesforce

Salesforce is a cloud-based CRM platform designed to help businesses manage customer relationships, sales activities, services, and overall business operations. It provides organizations with a centralized system to handle customer data and business processes efficiently.

Using Salesforce, companies can:

* Maintain customer information
* Monitor communication history
* Automate routine tasks
* Improve customer engagement
* Enhance sales performance

Since Salesforce operates on cloud technology, users can access the platform from anywhere with an internet connection.

Advantages of Cloud-Based Salesforce

* No software installation required
* Access from any location
* Instant updates and synchronization
* Secure storage of business data

---

2. Problems Solved by Salesforce

Before CRM platforms like Salesforce, many organizations depended on:

* Spreadsheets

These methods created several business challenges:

* Loss of customer information

* Unorganized business records

Salesforce addresses these issues by offering a unified platform to:

* Store customer data centrally
* Track business opportunities
* Improve communication
* Automate workflows
* Generate business reports and insights

4. Understanding CRM

CRM stands for Customer Relationship Management.

It is a system that helps organizations manage customer interactions and maintain business relationships effectively.

CRM platforms help businesses:

* Store customer records
* Track communication history
* Manage the sales lifecycle
* Improve customer support
* Build long-term customer relationships

Salesforce is one of the leading CRM solutions used globally.

Importance of CRM

Without CRM systems:

* Customer data may get misplaced
* Follow-ups can be missed
* Teams may become disorganized
* Sales performance can decrease

CRM solves these issues by organizing customer information and streamlining business processes.

5. Salesforce Business Workflow

The basic customer lifecycle in Salesforce is:

Lead → Contact → Opportunity → Customer

This flow explains how a person moves from being a potential customer to a successful client.

---

6. Difference Between Lead, Contact, and Opportunity

Lead

A Lead is an individual who has shown interest in a product or service.

At this stage:

* The customer is not yet verified
* The organization is collecting initial details

Example:

A student requesting information about college admissions.

---

Contact

A Contact is a verified individual connected to the business or organization.

At this stage:

* Communication has started
* Customer details are confirmed

Example:

A student discussing admission details with a counselor.

---

Opportunity

An Opportunity represents a potential business transaction or sale.

It helps organizations track:

* Revenue possibilities
* Success probability
* Sales progress

Example:

Admission process for a B.Tech program.

10. Salesforce Playground

Salesforce Playground is a practice environment provided by Salesforce for learning and experimentation.

It allows users to:

* Practice safely
* Build and test applications
* Complete Trailhead exercises
* Explore Salesforce features without affecting real company data

---

11. Creating a Trailhead Playground

Steps to create a new Playground:

1. Open Trailhead.
2. Click on the org name.
3. Select Create Playground.
4. Enter a name for the Playground.
5. Click Create.

The Playground will be ready for hands-on practice and learning activities.

---

12. Resetting Playground Password

If Playground Starter App is Available

1. Open Get Your Login Credentials.
2. View the username.
3. Click Reset My Password.
4. Open the email received.
5. Set and confirm a new password.

---

If Playground Starter App is Not Available

1. Launch the Playground.
2. Open Setup.
3. Search for Users.
4. Find your username.
5. Select the user and click Reset Password(s).
6. Use the email link to create a new password.

---

13. Managing Trailhead Playgrounds

Users can:

* Rename Playgrounds
* Disconnect existing orgs
* Connect Developer Edition orgs
* Create new Playgrounds

This can be done from the Manage Orgs section.

14. Important Salesforce Objects

Account

Represents a company or organization.

Example:

Engineering College

Steps to Create an Account

1. Open Sales App.
2. Go to Accounts.
3. Click New.
4. Enter account details.
5. Save the record.

---

Contact

Represents a person associated with an account.

Example:

Student or Parent

Steps to Create a Contact

1. Open Contacts related list.
2. Click New.
3. Enter personal details.
4. Save the record.

---

Opportunity

Represents a possible business deal or transaction.

Example:

Admission process or business proposal.

Steps to Create an Opportunity

1. Open the Account.

2. Navigate to Opportunities.

3. Click New.

4. Enter opportunity details such as:

   * Opportunity Name
   * Amount
   * Close Date
   * Stage

5. Save the record.

17. Difference Between Salesforce Admin and Developer

| Salesforce Admin                | Salesforce Developer                |
| ------------------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| Manages platform settings       | Develops custom applications        |
| Works mainly with configuration | Works with coding and customization |
| Creates reports and dashboards  | Builds custom functionality         |
| Handles users and permissions   | Writes Apex and Lightning code      |
| Focuses on administration       | Focuses on development              |
