Salesforce Summer Program – Day 15

Topic

Enterprise Data Management and Data Quality

Introduction

Modern enterprise systems such as Salesforce, ERP platforms, banking systems, healthcare applications, and university management systems depend heavily on data.

Every business process relies on data, including:

* Reports
* Dashboards
* Automation
* Approval workflows
* Notifications
* Analytics
* Business decisions

A common software engineering principle is:

“Garbage In, Garbage Out”

This means that if incorrect data enters a system, the outputs generated by that system will also become incorrect and unreliable.

Today's focus includes:

* Data Management
* Data Quality
* Data Migration
* Duplicate Prevention
* Enterprise Governance
* Data Reliability

These concepts are critical because enterprise systems are only as valuable as the quality of the data they contain.

---

What is Data Management?

Data Management is the process of collecting, storing, organizing, maintaining, protecting, and using data effectively throughout its lifecycle.

Enterprise organizations manage massive amounts of information daily.

Examples include:

* Student records
* Employee information
* Customer accounts
* Financial transactions
* Attendance records
* Academic results
* Product inventories

Without proper data management:

* Records become inconsistent
* Reports become inaccurate
* Automation becomes unreliable
* Business decisions become risky

Good data management ensures information remains:

* Accurate
* Consistent
* Complete
* Secure
* Accessible

Data Import and Export

Data Import

Data Import is the process of moving external data into Salesforce.

Examples:

* Importing students from Excel
* Importing employee records
* Migrating customer databases
* Importing historical data

Benefits

* Faster data entry
* Reduced manual work
* Large-scale processing
* Easier migration

Risks

* Duplicate records
* Missing fields
* Invalid values
* Incorrect field mapping

---

Data Export

Data Export is the process of extracting information from Salesforce.

Examples:

* Reporting
* Backup creation
* Auditing
* Analytics
* System migration

Benefits

* Backup and recovery
* Business analysis
* Regulatory compliance
* Data portability

---

Data Loader

Data Loader is a Salesforce tool designed for bulk data operations.

Organizations often need to process thousands of records simultaneously.

Data Loader supports:

* Insert
* Update
* Upsert
* Delete
* Export

Example

A college may need to import:

* 20,000 student records
* 5,000 course records
* 1,000 faculty records

Creating these manually would take weeks.

Data Loader can complete the operation within minutes.

Enterprise Advantages

* High-performance bulk processing
* Faster migration
* Automation compatibility
* Efficient large-scale operations

Bad Data Scenarios

Poor data quality creates serious business problems.

Scenario 1: Duplicate Student Records

Problem:

The same student appears multiple times.

Business Impact:

* Duplicate emails
* Incorrect attendance reports
* Confusing student records

---

Scenario 2: Missing Email Address

Problem:

Student email field is empty.

Business Impact:

* Important notifications fail
* Students miss announcements

---

Scenario 3: Wrong Department Assignment

Problem:

Student assigned to the wrong department.

Business Impact:

* Incorrect course allocation
* Inaccurate reporting

---

Scenario 4: Invalid Attendance Values

Problem:

Attendance recorded as 120%.

Business Impact:

* Reporting errors
* Incorrect academic decisions

---

Scenario 5: Duplicate Course Enrollment

Problem:

Student enrolled twice in the same course.

Business Impact:

* Scheduling conflicts
* Data inconsistency

---

Scenario 6: Incorrect Contact Information

Problem:

Invalid phone number stored.

Business Impact:

* Communication failures
* Emergency contact problems

---

Scenario 7: Missing Fee Data

Problem:

Payment information incomplete.

Business Impact:

* Financial reporting issues
* Incorrect balances

---

Scenario 8: Invalid Scholarship Records

Problem:

Wrong student receives scholarship approval.

Business Impact:

* Financial loss
* Student dissatisfaction

---

Scenario 9: Invalid Date Formats

Problem:

Different date formats used.

Examples:

```text
01/05/2025
2025-05-01
May 1, 2025
```

Business Impact:

* Import failures
* Reporting inconsistencies

---

Scenario 10: Missing Faculty Information

Problem:

Faculty records incomplete.

Business Impact:

* Scheduling issues
* Operational inefficiency

Data Migration Thinking

Scenario

A college currently stores information in Excel spreadsheets and decides to migrate to Salesforce.

Although migration sounds simple, it introduces several enterprise challenges.

---

Challenge 1: Duplicate Records

Multiple spreadsheets may contain the same student.

Result:

* Duplicate records
* Duplicate notifications
* Reporting inconsistencies

---

Challenge 2: Missing Data

Some records contain incomplete information.

Result:

* Incomplete profiles
* Communication failures

---

Challenge 3: Inconsistent Formats

Examples:

* Different date formats
* Different department naming conventions
* Inconsistent capitalization

Result:

* Import failures
* Data inconsistencies

---

Challenge 4: Invalid Data

Examples:

* Attendance greater than 100%
* Invalid email addresses
* Missing IDs

Result:

* System errors
* Unreliable reports

---

Challenge 5: Relationship Mapping

Students must be linked correctly to:

* Courses
* Departments
* Faculty
* Attendance records

Incorrect mapping may break system relationships.

---

Migration Best Practices

Before migration:

* Clean data
* Remove duplicates
* Standardize formats
* Validate records
* Test migrations

After migration:

* Verify imported data
* Check relationships
* Audit records
* Validate reporting accuracy

Duplicate Prevention Strategies

Duplicate data is one of the most common enterprise data quality issues.

Matching Rules

Salesforce compares records before saving.

Examples:

* Same email address
* Same phone number

Potential duplicates are identified automatically.

---

Duplicate Rules

The system can:

* Block duplicate creation
* Show warning messages

---

Validation Rules

Validation Rules ensure data follows business standards.

Examples:

* Attendance cannot exceed 100%
* Email must follow valid format
* Required IDs cannot be blank

---

Mandatory Fields

Important fields must be completed before saving.

Examples:

* Student ID
* Email
* Department
* Phone number

---

Regular Data Audits

Organizations periodically review records.

Benefits:

* Detect duplicates
* Correct inaccuracies
* Improve overall quality
